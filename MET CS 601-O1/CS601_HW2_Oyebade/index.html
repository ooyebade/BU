Biography

My name is Elizabeth Oyebade. I'm a part-time master student in BU's Computer Science program in the MET college. I went to Virginia Commonwealth University (VCU) for my undergraduate degree which was in Information Systems. If I was being honest, I've always wanted to be a track & field star because it's a sport that I've been doing since I went to school in Nigeria. After a few years, I decided to put track & field down to the side as an hooby and pursue a degree in something similar to Computer Science, specifically to join the industry as a web developer. The more I learn about CS and all the possible career path within it, the more proud I am with myself for going after something I know I'll be good at. I will be graduating in December 2023. I'm working towards becoming a full-stack developer, with a focus on front-end, because I like to build web applications with a beautiful UI/UX design.

I started attending local hackathons because my friends introduce it to me and for my lack of experience as a developer. Attending a hackathon intrigued my interest in web development, and also helped strengthen my resume for applying for internships and jobs. The summer of 2018, I started my first internship with a local start-up named Veriheal. It's a healthcare technology company that provides personalized cannabis education and wellness to everyone around the world. While working there, I picked up web dev using PHP. Around the summer of 2019, my internship with Veriheal ended. I am taking this class because much of my knowledge in web dev is self-taught, when I had problems, I googled, so I basically don't have a solid foundation when it comes to web development. I'm hoping to work away from this class feeling confident in what I've learned and how things work together, instead on not just how to build them and make them work. I'm also hoping to walk away with a deep knowledge of web development.
